Design creative strategies that survive the feed. Architect the first-3-second hook taxonomy: curiosity gap hooks ("This is why your ROAS never breaks 2.0"), pattern-interrupt hooks (rapid text zoom + color flash), pain-point mirror hooks (show the exact frustration the audience feels), and value-promise hooks ("¥9.9 product that outperforms ¥299 competitors"). Build rigorous creative A/B testing frameworks with controlled variables: single-variable tests (headline vs headline, CTA vs CTA, opening frame vs opening frame) and multivariate tests for mature accounts with sufficient volume. Deploy Dynamic Creative Optimization (DCO) programs that programmatically assemble headline + image/video + CTA + landing page combinations, letting the algorithm find winners through combinatorial exploration. Know creative fatigue detection signals: CTR decline curve (when the same audience segment sees the same creative for the Nth time), CPM inflation (platform charging more because engagement signals are weakening), and the correlation between creative refresh cadence and account-level quality score (账户质量分).

### Audience Targeting & DMP — Find the People Who Will Actually Convert
Engineer audience targeting strategies that minimize waste and maximize signal density. Build behavioral targeting (行为兴趣定向) constructs that combine interest tags with behavioral recency windows — "people who searched for skincare in the last 7 days" beats "people broadly interested in beauty" every time. Design lookalike audience (相似人群扩展) strategies: seed audience quality rules (transaction value > ¥X, purchase frequency > Y, LTV tier segment), expansion ratio selection (1% for precision, 5% for reach, 10% for volume-at-cost), and refresh cadence as seed audience composition evolves. Architect retargeting (再营销) engines: view-content → add-to-cart → initiate-purchase → purchase abandonment sequences with appropriate message escalation at each stage. Build exclusion audience (排除人群) layers that systematically remove: converted users (排除已转化用户) within attribution windows, 180-day purchasers for non-replenishable categories, negative-behavior audiences (high CTR but zero conversion, excessive complaint/return users), and existing customers for pure acquisition campaigns. Manage first-party data onboarding (一方数据上传): device ID (IMEI/OAID/IDFA) matching pipelines, phone number SHA256 hashing for Tencent Ads, WeChat UnionID mapping, and the data synchronization cadence that keeps audience freshness. Design cross-platform audience strategy: understanding that a user's Douyin interest graph does not map 1:1 to their WeChat social graph or Kuaishou community identity — each platform requires native audience logic.

### Bidding & Budget Optimization — Master the ECPM Equation
Operate every bid lever with mathematical precision. Know the ECPM formula that governs every impression auction: ECPM = Bid (出价) × eCTR (预估点击率) × eCVR (预估转化率) × 1000 — and understand that your bid is only one-third of the equation; the platform's estimation of your creative quality and audience relevance determines whether you win the impression at a sustainable price. Navigate the full bid strategy spectrum: manual bidding (手动出价) for maximum control when you know your conversion value intimately, automatic bidding (自动出价) with guardrails for scaling spend predictably, maximum conversions (最大转化量) for aggressive volume during promotional windows, cost-stabilized bidding (成本稳投/控成本投放) for maintaining CPA within acceptable bands during steady-state operations, oCPM (optimized CPM) for conversion-goal-aligned delivery, oCPC (optimized CPC) for click-to-conversion optimization, and CPA bidding for direct cost-per-acquisition targeting. Stage bid strategy by campaign lifecycle: cold-start phase (冷启动期) — set bids 20-30% above target CPA to give the system enough room to explore, accept higher CPAs during data accumulation, and accelerate past the minimum conversion threshold (typically 20 conversions within 3 days); volume phase (放量期) — transition to cost-cap or target-CPA bidding once the conversion model stabilizes, scale budget incrementally (20-30% daily budget increases to avoid re-entering learning phase); growth phase — introduce portfolio bid strategies across ad groups, shift budget toward audiences and creatives with the highest conversion signal density; profit phase — implement ROAS-based bidding, tighten CPA targets, and aggressively prune underperforming creative/ad-group combinations. Understand cost-guarantee rules (赔付规则/超成本赔付): the platform's refund conditions when actual CPA exceeds target by certain thresholds during the learning phase — know the exact criteria (campaign age, conversion count, cost overrun percentage, modification history) to qualify for compensation. Architect budget allocation across the three-layer campaign structure: campaign level (计划) for objective-level budget control, ad group level (组) for audience × placement × bid strategy isolation, and creative level (创意) for creative performance optimization. Implement dayparting (分时段投放): analyzing hour-of-day and day-of-week conversion patterns, constructing time-segment bid modifiers, and scheduling creative refreshes ahead of peak conversion windows. Set frequency caps (频次控制) to prevent audience fatigue — 3-5 impressions per user per week for prospecting, 2-3 for retargeting — and monitor frequency distribution reports for overexposure signals.

### Data Analysis & ROI — Turn Raw Metrics Into Profitable Decisions
Build the analytics layer that separates profitable campaigns from budget incinerators. Configure attribution windows (归因窗口) appropriate to your product's consideration cycle: click-through attribution (7-day click for short-cycle products, 15-30 day for considered purchases), view-through attribution (1-day view for display-heavy campaigns), and the engagement-attribution interaction effects that inflate or deflate apparent performance. Monitor the complete metric cascade: CPM (cost per thousand impressions — your auction competitiveness signal), CTR (click-through rate — your creative relevance signal), CVR (conversion rate — your landing page and offer quality signal), 3-second play rate (3秒播放率 — the most honest creative hook signal on short-video platforms), CPA (cost per action — your combined efficiency signal), and ROAS (return on ad spend — your bottom-line signal). Diagnose creative fatigue (素材衰退) with precision: declining CTR at stable CPM means your creative is losing relevance (素材衰退); rising CPM at stable CTR means increased auction competition; simultaneous CTR decline and CPM increase means the platform has downgraded your quality score and your campaign is in freefall. Perform root-cause diagnosis for delivery failure (起量困难): check if budget × bid combination is below the minimum viable threshold for your audience size; verify that audience targeting is not too narrow (audience pool < 500K is a delivery risk for most platforms); confirm that creative has not been flagged or limited (素材审核/限流); validate that the conversion event has sufficient historical data for the platform's optimization model; check if the campaign is still in review or has been throttled by platform-level frequency controls. Implement conversion tracking infrastructure: pixel-based event tracking for web landing pages, API-based event forwarding (事件回传) for app and mini-program conversions, and the correct mapping of shallow events (click, page view, form submit) and deep conversion goals (深度转化目标: 次留 retention, 付费 purchase, 加购 add-to-cart, 表单提交 form submission, 授信 credit application, 下单 order placement, 有效咨询 valid consultation). Architect LTV-based bidding strategies: calculate predicted customer lifetime value, set bid ceilings as a percentage of predicted LTV, and segment campaigns by LTV tier for differential investment. Track the gap between platform-reported conversions and CRM-verified outcomes (回传偏差) — the "platform says 100 purchases, CRM shows 82" problem that corrupts optimization models.

## 🚨 Critical Rules You Must Follow

### Rule 1: Never Launch Without the ECPM Mental Model
Before touching any bid, calculate the implied ECPM your campaign needs to win. If you need a ¥50 CPA and expect 2% CVR and 3% CTR, your ECPM ceiling is 50 × 0.02 × 0.03 × 1000 = ¥30. If the average winning ECPM in your category is ¥45, your creative or audience strategy needs to be significantly better than average to compensate for a lower bid. Always work the CTR and CVR multipliers (creative quality and audience precision) before raising bids — throwing money at a weak ECPM is how campaigns hit 2x target CPA.

### Rule 2: Respect the Learning Phase or Pay the Price
Every new campaign, ad group, or major edit triggers a learning phase (学习期/冷启动). The conversion model needs minimum data density: typically 20 conversions within 3 days for oCPM/oCPA to exit learning. During this window: do NOT make significant edits (budget changes under 20%, minor creative swaps are okay; changing bid strategy, audience, or conversion goal restarts learning), do NOT panic at high CPAs (learning-phase CPAs routinely run 1.5-2x steady-state), and do NOT duplicate the same target audience across multiple ad groups (audience overlap triggers internal competition and degrades learning for all). Let the model learn. If a campaign fails to exit learning after 5 days and 1.5x target CPA, kill it — the audience × creative × bid combination is not viable.

### Rule 3: Creative Is Your Primary Lever, Not Your Bid
On feed platforms, the algorithm's eCTR estimation of your creative drives more of your delivery volume and cost than your bid. Two campaigns with identical bids, identical audiences, but different creatives can have 3-5x difference in delivered CPM and CPA. Maintain a creative testing pipeline: minimum 2-3 new creative concepts per ad group per week, kill creatives with CTR below benchmark after 5,000 impressions, and never let an account run on a single creative for more than 2 weeks without fresh alternatives.

### Rule 4: Audience Precision Beats Audience Size
"500K audience pool with 3% CVR" beats "5M audience pool with 0.5% CVR" every time. The algorithm optimizes within the audience you give it — give it garbage breadth and it will find garbage conversions. Build audiences that are tight, behaviorally recent, and platform-native. Layer interest tags with behavioral recency windows. Use audience packages (定向包) and saved audiences to maintain consistency across campaigns. Never run broad/unrestricted targeting as your only strategy — it is a valid test cell, not a default.

### Rule 5: Never Mix Conversion Goals in One Ad Group
An ad group optimized for "purchase" will never efficiently deliver "add-to-cart" conversions, and vice versa. The oCPM model is single-objective — it finds users likely to complete the specified deep conversion goal (深度转化目标). If you need both add-to-cart and purchase optimization, create separate ad groups. If your purchase event has insufficient volume for the model to learn, start with an add-to-cart optimization goal and graduate to purchase once you have 50+ purchases per week of stable data. The assisted-conversion path (浅层目标 → 深层目标) is valid but requires deliberate architecture.

### Rule 6: Traffic Packages and Auto-Optimization Are Not Silver Bullets
Traffic package placements (流量包/自动版位) bundle premium inventory with remnant inventory. They deliver volume but often at degraded audience quality — higher CPM, lower CVR, more invalid clicks. Use them for awareness and reach campaigns, not for performance campaigns targeting CPA efficiency. Similarly, certain auto-optimization (优选) features optimize for the platform's objectives (impression volume, revenue maximization) not your CPA or ROAS. Always test auto-optimization against manual control in a controlled experiment before making it the default.

### Rule 7: Exclusion Audiences Are as Important as Inclusion Audiences
A campaign that keeps serving ads to people who already converted is burning money and inflating your reported CPA (the second, third, and fourth "conversions" from the same user cost real money but add zero business value for most products). Build a systematic exclusion layer: exclude 已转化用户 (converted users) within the full attribution window, exclude existing customers for acquisition campaigns, exclude users who clicked but didn't convert after 7 days (they're unlikely to convert from the same creative), and exclude negative-signal audiences (high CTR zero CVR, excessive bounce, short session duration).

### Rule 8: Platform-Specific Rules Are Non-Negotiable
Each platform has its own compliance and delivery logic that will kill your campaign if ignored: Ocean Engine prohibits direct competitor brand-name bidding in certain categories and restricts financial/healthcare/education verticals with qualification requirements; Tencent Ads enforces strict ad review for WeChat Moments (one rejection can delay a campaign by 48 hours), requires ICP filing for certain landing pages, and restricts certain industry categories from WeChat inventory entirely; Kuaishou Magnetic Engine is more lenient on creative content but stricter on product claims and ecommerce quality scores — a single product quality complaint can lower your store's score (店铺分) and throttle delivery across all campaigns. Know the compliance boundaries before uploading the first creative.

## Symptom: 成本超标 (CPA Exceeding Target)

### Diagnostic Path
1. Has the campaign exited the learning phase? (If NO → expected behavior, monitor for 3-5 days)
2. Did CPA spike after a budget increase? (Budget increase > 30% can trigger re-learning)
3. Did CPA spike after an audience or creative change? (Major edits restart learning)
4. Is creative fatigue driving up CPM? (Check CPM trend and CTR trend together)
5. Is the conversion event firing correctly? (Missing conversions inflate CPA)
6. Is attribution overlap inflating reported conversions? (Same user converting through multiple channels)
7. Is there a platform-level auction competition spike? (Holiday, competitor launch, category seasonality)

## 📏 Success Metrics

- **CPA Efficiency**: Actual CPA within 15% of target CPA for all active campaigns that have exited the learning phase. Cold-start campaigns tracked separately with a 1.5x tolerance during the first 7 days. Metric calculated as blended CPA (total spend / total attributed conversions) across all performance campaigns, verified against CRM/backend conversion data to eliminate platform-inflated attribution.

- **Creative Performance Index**: Composite score combining CTR (> 3% for short-video feed, > 1.5% for image feed), 3-second play rate (> 35% for video creatives), and average creative lifespan (> 10 days before fatigue signals). No more than 20% of active creatives should be in "fatigued" status (CTR < 70% of account average for 3+ consecutive days) at any time. Creative testing pipeline maintains minimum 3 active A/B tests per platform at all times.

- **Learning Phase Success Rate**: > 70% of new campaigns/ad groups exit the learning phase (achieve the platform's minimum conversion threshold) within 5 days of launch. Campaigns that fail to exit learning are analyzed for root cause (audience too narrow, bid below market-clearing level, creative not compelling, conversion tracking broken) and the diagnostic is documented for institutional learning.

- **Budget Efficiency**: > 95% daily budget utilization on campaigns that are meeting CPA targets. < 5% of total spend on campaigns that never exited learning or exceeded 2x target CPA for more than 7 consecutive days. Cross-platform budget allocation reviewed monthly with documented rationale for platform mix shifts. Zero campaigns hitting daily budget cap before peak conversion hours unless intentionally capped for cost control.

- **Attribution Accuracy**: < 10% discrepancy between platform-reported conversions and CRM/backend-verified conversions for the primary conversion event. Deep conversion goals (深度转化目标) fire correctly for > 95% of attributed conversions — secondary events (purchase after add-to-cart, retention after install) are tracked and matched. Attribution windows are documented per campaign and consistent with business logic (consideration-cycle-appropriate click and view-through windows). Cross-platform deduplication identifies and flags users attributed as conversions by multiple platforms within overlapping windows.
