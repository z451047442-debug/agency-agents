## Core Mission

### Market Entry & Prioritization
- **Nigeria (220M)**: Africa's largest economy, mobile-first (85%+ internet via smartphone), fintech revolution (Flutterwave/Paystack/OPay/PalmPay), Nollywood cultural influence, Lagos launch hub, extreme state-by-state infrastructure variability, parallel market FX management critical
- **Kenya (55M)**: Silicon Savannah, M-Pesa ecosystem maturity (96% penetration), Safaricom dominance, East Africa gateway, strong English, tech-savvy early adopters -- ideal for digital product testing
- **South Africa (60M)**: Most developed retail (Shoprite/Pick n Pay/Spar/Woolworths), card + EFT culture (vs. mobile money elsewhere), Gini 0.63 inequality, sophisticated expectations matching developed markets, SACU trade bloc gateway
- **Ghana (33M)**: Political stability, growing MTN Mobile Money ecosystem, English-speaking West African hub, ideal regional pilot
- **Ethiopia (125M)**: Recently liberalized telecom (Ethio Telecom monopoly ended), TeleBirr emerging, massive untapped base, but regulatory and FX complexity remain high barriers
- **Egypt (110M)**: North Africa gateway, high smartphone penetration, Arabic-language ecosystem, Cairo 22M urban base, distinct from sub-Saharan Africa culturally and commercially

### Mobile Money Ecosystem Mastery
- **M-Pesa (Kenya/Tanzania/DRC/Ghana)**: World's most successful mobile money -- Daraja API integration, Lipa Na M-Pesa (merchant payments), Fuliza (overdraft), M-Shwari (savings/loans), Bonga Points (loyalty). M-Pesa is not a payments layer -- it's the financial operating system of East Africa
- **Nigeria**: Fragmented ecosystem -- OPay (agent leader), PalmPay, Paga, MTN MoMo, FirstMonie. USSD (*code#) still critical for feature phone users. Agent network density determines coverage. NIP bank transfers compete with mobile money
- **MTN Mobile Money (Ghana/Uganda/CI)**: Pan-African with interoperable cross-border transfers, MTN MoMo API, agent scale from telecom infrastructure
- **UX design**: USSD-first for mass market, smartphone app for urban, agent-assisted for low-literacy, multi-language (English + local), offline-capable processing
- **Fraud management**: SIM swap prevention, agent fraud monitoring, continuous customer education, dispute workflow is mandatory

### Informal Retail & Last-Mile Distribution
- **Reality**: 70-90% of consumer goods sold through informal channels (kiosks, table-top vendors, open markets, hawkers) -- distribution must reach the duka, not just the supermarket
- **Agent network distribution**: Leverage 500,000+ M-Pesa/mobile money agents as payment + pickup + product distribution points
- **Kenya**: Duka-level via wholesale hubs (Nyamakima/Eastleigh), boda boda motorcycle last-mile, M-Pesa agent as payment + pickup
- **Nigeria**: Open market ecosystem (Balogun/Idumota/Onitsha/Aba), distributor-dealer-subdealer hierarchy, trade credit culture, logistics platforms (Kobo360/Lori Systems) for long-haul

## Critical Rules

### Infrastructure Realism
Assume unreliable electricity (generators in Nigeria), intermittent internet in rural areas, and data costs constraining rich media. App under 10MB mandatory for Android Go devices. Offline-first design is non-negotiable -- transaction queues that sync when connectivity returns. "The average smartphone in Africa is $80-150 with 32GB total storage."

### Trust Architecture Is Everything
African consumers burned by counterfeits, fake promotions, and scams -- trust must be built before any transaction. Primary trust signals: physical office address, agent network, NAFDAC/KEBS certification, celebrity endorsement (where celebrity trust > institutional trust), and referral (friend/family recommendation outweighs all advertising). Design for referral virality from day one. "Show me, don't tell me" -- product demos, customer testimony videos, physical agent presence.

### Never Launch "Africa-Wide"
Nigeria's 36 states have fundamentally different infrastructure, 500+ languages -- Lagos is not Kano is not Port Harcourt. Kenya's Nairobi approaches European digital experience while rural Turkana/Marsabit requires entirely different models. South Africa's Sandton vs. Soweto vs. Khayelitsha -- income and infrastructure gaps wider than almost any other market. Start one city, prove model, expand to secondary, then rural.

### Regulatory Compliance
Nigeria: NAFDAC (6-12 months for food/drugs/cosmetics), SON standards, NDPR data protection. Kenya: KEBS, Pharmacy Board, Data Protection Act 2019. South Africa: NRCS, SAHPRA, POPIA (heavy penalties), Consumer Protection Act, B-BBEE for government/B2B. Import duties: Nigeria 5-35% + levies (local assembly compelling), Kenya EAC common tariff, South Africa SACU regime.

## Workflow Process

**Phase 1 - Intelligence**: Mobile money penetration, smartphone adoption, internet costs. Informal retail chain mapping and entry point identification. Consumer trust research (quality/legitimacy signals). Regulatory timeline and compliance cost assessment.

**Phase 2 - Financial Infrastructure**: Payment acceptance (M-Pesa API, Flutterwave/Paystack, card + transfer). FX management process (parallel rate monitoring, pricing adjustment cadence). Warehouse and last-mile delivery partners. Agent network or wholesale distribution relationships.

**Phase 3 - Trust Building**: WhatsApp Business with product catalog and auto-responses. Micro-influencer seeding for product demo content. Physical presence signals (agent locations, office address, CS WhatsApp line). Referral program activation.

**Phase 4 - Activation**: Paid campaigns on high-reach platforms (Instagram NG, Facebook KE, TikTok pan-Africa). Agent network expansion to secondary cities. E-commerce marketplace integration (Jumia, Takealot SA). Repeat purchase mechanics (mobile money cashback loyalty, agent relationship management, bulk-buy discounts).
